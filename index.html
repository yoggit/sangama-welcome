Where two worlds meet to make something new

Every day, one creative challenge that blends an art with a science — draw a city built on the Fibonacci sequence, write a poem that explains gravity, design an instrument from a hexagon.

Sangama (Kannada: confluence) is where two worlds meet. It's a catalyst — kids turn what they know into something they make, building the habit of connecting ideas across subjects instead of just memorising them.

How it works

One small challenge a day — create, reflect, and keep what you make.

1

Get today's challenge

A fresh art × science mash-up, picked just for your child — never the same one twice.

2

Make it

Draw it on the canvas, write the idea on the pad, or both. Shapes, colours, undo — a real little studio.

3

Keep it

Save it to a personal sketchbook and grow a streak. Every creation stays, so they can look back and retry.

Why it helps

🌉

Knowledge into creation

School teaches the facts; Sangama makes kids use them — the surest sign they truly understand.

🔀

Cross-domain thinking

Every challenge fuses two subjects, training the rare skill of connecting ideas that usually live apart.

🎨

Draw & write studio

A freehand canvas with shapes, colours, fill and undo, plus a writing pad — express the idea any way it fits.

🤔

Reflect, don't grade

A gentle self-check — did you connect both worlds? is it clever? is it clear? — builds judgement, not pressure.

🖼️

Try first, then peek

A worked "finished idea" is there if they're stuck — but only after they've opened the hints and had a real go.

📴

Private & offline

Ad-free, no tracking, works with no connection — and a child's creations stay in their own browser, never uploaded.
